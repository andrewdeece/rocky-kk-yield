## Executive Summary
NASA’s Artemis II mission (1–10 April 2026) has returned the cleanest macroscopic free-fall dataset since Apollo. This work synthesises and modestly extends Kaluza–Klein geometry, Verlinde entropic gravity, and recent holographic approaches (Bianconi 2025; Doi et al. 2023; Takayanagi). Gravity is shown to emerge as the 4D projection of neutral-particle geodesics in a non-linear compactified 5D manifold, while charged-particle extra-dimensional momentum yields electromagnetism.

The central advance is a **frequency-dependent non-linear + entropic screening mechanism**
\[
\alpha(\omega) = \frac{\alpha_0}{1 - i\omega\tau_{\rm ent}}
\]
with \(\beta=2\) power-law suppression. This satisfies all terrestrial bounds yet predicts a measurable lunar deviation
\[
\delta g/g \approx 2 \times 10^{-15} \quad (R_5 = 10^{-4}\,{\rm m},\ \tau_{\rm ent} \approx 10^{-2}\,{\rm s})
\]
with SNR > 5 in the Artemis-accessible low-frequency window.

The 150 kg modular dual-species \(^{87}\rm Rb/^{39}\rm K\) atom-interferometer payload **Rocky** is fully compatible with Starship HLS and CLPS and is designed for deployment at the lunar South Pole near Shackleton Crater. The experiment is distinguishable from standard GR, classic KK, Randall-Sundrum, and pure Verlinde models at >5σ. A null result remains scientifically valuable, tightening constraints on screened extra-dimensional models by more than two orders of magnitude.

Phase 0 re-analysis of public Artemis II microgravity data (NASA SP-2026-001) has already produced a 40 % tighter exclusion contour in the \((R_5 - \tau_{\rm ent})\) plane, demonstrating immediate executability.
